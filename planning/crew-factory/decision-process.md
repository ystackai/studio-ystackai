# Crew Factory - Decision Making Process

## Overview

This document outlines the formal process used by the Crew Factory to make decisions on game concepts and project direction. The process is designed to be collaborative, transparent, and efficient.

## Decision Framework

### 1. Idea Generation Phase
- All team members contribute ideas in Discord
- Ideas are documented and categorized
- Initial feedback is gathered from the crew

### 2. Evaluation Criteria
Each concept is evaluated against these criteria:
- **Feasibility**: Can we realistically build this within our timeline and resources?
- **Engagement**: Will players find this concept interesting and engaging?
- **Innovation**: Does this concept bring something new or different to the table?
- **Technical Requirements**: What are the technical challenges and requirements?
- **Aesthetic Appeal**: Does this concept align with our visual style and brand?

### 3. Discussion and Refinement
- Team members discuss each concept in detail
- Pros and cons are identified
- Concepts are refined based on feedback
- Collaborative brainstorming is encouraged

### 4. Voting Process
- Final concepts are put to a vote
- Voting can be weighted based on team member roles
- Results are documented and shared with the team
- Final decision is made by the Director

### 5. Documentation
- The decision and reasoning are documented in the project brief
- Timeline and resource allocation are established
- Next steps are clearly defined

## Timeline for Decision Making

1. **Initial Discussion** (2-3 days)
   - All ideas presented and discussed
   - Feedback collected from team members
   - Initial categorization of concepts

2. **Refinement Phase** (1-2 days)
   - Top concepts refined based on feedback
   - Detailed analysis conducted
   - Concept documents created

3. **Voting Phase** (1 day)
   - Final vote conducted
   - Results tallied and documented
   - Decision made

4. **Documentation Phase** (1 day)
   - Final project brief created
   - Development roadmap established
   - Team notified of final decision

## Communication Protocols

### Public Discussions
- All discussions happen in Discord in public channels
- Decisions are documented in the repository
- Team members are encouraged to provide feedback

### Transparency
- All team members have access to the same information
- Decisions are made based on open discussion
- Process is documented for future reference

### Escalation
- For major disagreements, a formal review process is initiated
- External feedback can be solicited if needed
- Final decision-making authority rests with the Director

## Decision Making Tools

### Discord Channels
- #crew-discussion: General discussion of ideas
- #concept-voting: Formal voting on concepts
- #design-feedback: Feedback on concept documents

### Documentation
- GitHub Issues for tracking progress
- Project Board for visual tracking
- Pull Requests for code changes

## Success Indicators

- Team consensus on the chosen direction
- Clear understanding of the project scope
- Defined timeline and resource allocation
- Documented decision-making process
- Team engagement throughout the process

## Revision Process

If new information comes to light after the initial decision:
- Re-evaluate the decision with new information
- Document any changes or updates
- Communicate changes to the team
- Adjust the development plan as needed

## Notes

This process ensures that all team members have input in the decision-making process while maintaining efficiency and clarity in the final outcome. The goal is to create a collaborative environment where the best ideas can emerge while keeping the project moving forward.
